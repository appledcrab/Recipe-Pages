Beef Pares

Cuisine:Filipino (Street Food)

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Calories: 400 per serving

Total Time:3 hours

Prep Time: 1 hour 30 minutes


Beef pares is a Filipino dish that is commonly found in diners and street vendors. Best paired with rice, this is a savory street dish that packs a flavor punch. The tender beef cooked in a delicious spice blend makes it a hit! Enjoy!

Instructions:

  1. Boil the brisket about 1 ½ hours until tender (depends on thickness of meat). Slow cooking is best.
  2. Blend the soy sauce, black pepper, and sugar. Marinade the brisket in this mixture for 30 minutes.
  3. Saute the ginger, garlic, and onion. While doing this, drain the marinade from the beef and saute the beef for 5-10 minutes. Add the beef broth and simmer.
  4. Add remaining marinade and simmer for 5 more minutes. Add star anise.
  5. Once the meat is very tender, dissolve the flour in the water and mix into beef while stirring and simmer until flour taste is gone(this is for a stickier sauce).
  6. Garnish with garlic and scallions as desired. Serve with rice.
