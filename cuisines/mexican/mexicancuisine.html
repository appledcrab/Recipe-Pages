Sopes

Cuisine:Mexican

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Calories: 300 per serving

Total Time:55 minutes

Prep Time:40 minutes


Sopes are a popular street snack in Mexico. The corn flour shell acts a little bowl that can be piled high with whatever toppings you like. Our version brings you the best combination of protein, cheese, vegetables, and flavor! Enjoy!

#StreetFood #Entree

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk the masa harina and salt. Stir in the water and lard. Knead dough until smooth (but not sticky), then form it into a ball and rest it for 5 minutes.
  2. Place dough on cutting board, flatten, and cut into 12 pieces like you would cut a pizza.
  3. Roll each piece of dough into a ball, then flatten into a 3 ½ inches wide disk. Transfer to baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Heat skillet to 350°F. Cook 4 sopes at a time for 60 seconds, then flip and cook for 30 more seconds.
  5. Transfer sopes to flat surface and let cool for 45 seconds. Pinch each sope around the edges to create a ½ inch thick rim.
  6. Put vegetable oil in skillet and heat to 350°F. Fry 4-6 sopes at a time for 1-2 minutes on each side. Transfer to baking sheet lined with paper towels.
  7. Spread a layer of refried beans on each sope. Top with meat, lettuce, onions, avocado, salsa, crema, cheese and any other desired toppings. Serve warm.
