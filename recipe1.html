Chicken and Waffles: A Soul Food Classic

Chicken and waffles is a timeless Southern culinary classic. While its origins are disputed (did it come from Pennsylvania Dutch families or southern slaves?), it is unquestionably a soul food staple today. Our recipe brings you the joys of crispy, juicy chicken, with crispy waffles and a kick of heat! Enjoy!

Got Everything You Need?




















Ingredients:

For chicken:

8 boneless chicken thighs

2 cups buttermilk

2 ½ cups flour

1 tbsp seasoned salt

1 tsp paprika

1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

1 tsp ground dried thyme

½ tsp cayenne pepper

¼ cup milk

Vegetable oil for frying

For waffles:

2 cups flour

¼ cup sugar

1 tbsp baking powder

½ tsp kosher salt

1 ½ cups milk

1 tbsp vanilla extract

4 large egg whites and 2 egg yolks

1 melted stick of salted butter

1 or 2 chopped jalapenos

For sauce:

1 cup pancake syrup

¼ cup bourbon

½ stick salted butter

¼ tsp cayenne pepper

Directions:

1: Rinse chicken and cover with 1 ¾ cups of buttermilk. Soak in fridge overnight.
2: Remove from fridge, leave chicken out for 30 minutes.
3. Preheat oven to 360°F. Thoroughly mix flour, seasoned salt, paprika, pepper, thyme and cayenne in large bowl.
4. Combine milk and remaining buttermilk in small bowl. Pour into flour mixture and mix until lumpy.
5. Heat 2 inches of vegeable oil in deep skillet to 365°F.
6. Thoroughly coat the chicken in the breading and add to skillet 3/4 pieces at a time.
7. For each batch, cover and cook 5-7 minutes. Flip and cook 3-5 minutes longer, making sure it doesn't get too brown.
8. Transfer chicken to baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes to finish cooking the meat.
9. For waffles: while waffle iron preheats, sift flour, sugar, baking powder and salt in bowl.
10. Whisk the milk, yolks, and vanilla in another bowl. Pour over the dry mix and gently stir until half mixed.
11. Add the melted butter and jalapenos and continue gently mixing until combined.
12. In another bowl, whisk the 4 egg whites until stiff. Slowly fold into batter.
13. Cook batter in waffle iron until golden and crispy.
14. For sauce: Boil syrup and bourbon in pot, simmer for 3-4 minutes.
15. Turn off heat and whisk in the butter 1 tsp at a time, followed by the cayenne pepper.
